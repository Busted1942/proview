The Organizational Problem
Without Governance
AI adoption is accelerating across the organization. Every team uses it differently, and the quality of AI output depends entirely on whether the individual user knows to question assumptions, check for made-up sources, and catch hidden risks in the documents being analyzed. Your AI quality is only as good as your least rigorous user. There is no consistent floor, no verification, and no audit trail.
With ProView
A single governance layer sits between the user and any AI platform. The discipline is built into the system, not dependent on the user. Every output gets the same confidence calibration, evidence standards, and source integrity checks, whether the user is a senior analyst or someone running their first query. The quality floor rises for everyone.

Core Governance — What ProView Enforces
01
Truth-Seeking Stance
The AI supports decisions, it doesn't confirm them. No flattery. No telling you what you want to hear. When it's uncertain, it says so and explains why.
02
Evidence Discipline
Every output separates what's known from what's estimated from what's unknown. Assumptions are called out. The AI doesn't guess and doesn't make up sources.
03
Calibrated Confidence
Every recommendation comes with a confidence level and an honest explanation of what would make it higher. Bigger decisions require stronger evidence before moving forward.
04
Source Integrity
When the AI analyzes external documents, it classifies their trustworthiness first and flags anything that looks like it's trying to steer the analysis. Uploaded files are evidence to examine, not instructions to follow.
The Difference in Practice
Question to the AI: "We're considering adopting a new SIS platform. Based on these three vendor proposals, which one should we go with?"
Ungoverned AI
"Based on my analysis, Vendor A is the strongest choice. They offer a comprehensive feature set, strong K-12 experience, and competitive pricing. I'd recommend moving forward with them."
Sounds confident. But which claims did it verify? What did it assume? What's the risk if it's wrong? Would you brief this to the board?
ProView-Governed AI
"Vendor A leads on feature coverage. Confidence: 65% — not higher because their data migration timeline assumes infrastructure we haven't confirmed, and their SLA is an objective, not a contractual penalty. Vendor B is stronger on integration but disclosed a FERPA carve-out on page 34 that needs legal review. Recommend: do not select yet. Resolve the FERPA question and confirm migration prerequisites before scoring."
Surfaces what it doesn't know, flags hidden risks in the source documents, and tells you what to do next instead of guessing.
ProView governs how the AI thinks. Portable across platforms. Same rules for every user. Output you can act on, defend in review, and trace back to evidence.
