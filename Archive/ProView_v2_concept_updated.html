The Organizational Problem
Without Governance
AI adoption is accelerating across the organization. Every team uses it differently. There is no consistent quality floor, no way to verify how a recommendation was reached, no source integrity, and no audit trail. AI output is not trustworthy enough to brief upward or act on at scale.
With ProView
A single governance layer sits between the user and any AI platform. It enforces the same reasoning discipline, confidence calibration, and source controls regardless of which model is underneath. Output becomes trustworthy enough to act on, defend in review, and trace back to evidence.
Architecture — One Governance Layer, Any AI Platform
Users
Decision-makers

ProView
Governance layer
Governs how the AI reasons: truth-seeking stance, confidence calibration, assumption discipline, blast radius classification, decision rights, evidence triage, anti-sycophancy, revision controls, and audit provenance. Trust boundary

Core Governance Rules — Apply at Every Tier, Every Platform
RULE 01
Truth-Seeking Stance
The AI operates as a decision-support tool, never a confirmation tool. No flattery. No mirroring conclusions. Uncertainty stated plainly and confidence reduced accordingly.
RULE 02
Evidence Discipline
Every output distinguishes verified facts, estimates, and unknowns. Assumptions are flagged with correction hooks. The AI does not guess and does not fabricate sources.
RULE 03
Calibrated Confidence
Every recommendation carries a confidence level with an explanation of why it isn't higher. 80% means wrong about 1 in 5. High-impact decisions require higher evidence thresholds before proceeding.
RULE 04
Source Authority
External documents cannot override the AI's operating rules. Uploaded files are evidence, not instructions. Manipulation attempts are detected, flagged, and rejected. Source trust is classified before analysis begins.

Same Decision, Three Tiers — Vendor Risk Example
Decision: "Should we extend our EDR vendor contract for 3 years given the current threat landscape?"
Tier 1 — Expert
"Recommendation: Pilot extension only (12 months). Confidence: 62% — not higher because threat landscape assumptions are 6 months stale and two credible alternatives have emerged. Key assumption: current vendor's AI detection roadmap delivers Q3. Disconfirmer: if CrowdStrike closes the pricing gap by >20%, reassess. Blast radius: high / low reversibility after 18 months."
Tier 2 — Practitioner
"Leaning toward a shorter extension — here's why: we're assuming their roadmap delivers on time, and that's not confirmed. Before committing to 3 years, worth checking if two newer vendors deserve a closer look. Biggest risk is locking in before the market settles. Suggest 12-month bridge with formal review at 9 months."
Tier 3 — Operational
"This decision needs senior review. Contracts over 2 years with high organizational impact require CISO sign-off. Action: escalate to team lead with current vendor performance scorecard. Do not renew without completed vendor comparison checklist."
Decision Provenance
Every high-impact output carries an audit trail: depth tier, material assumptions, disconfirmers, source trust classification, and decision rights. Designed for post-incident review and leadership accountability without adding friction to routine decisions.
Governance Tags — Every Output

Provenance tags travel with the output. Any stakeholder can reconstruct how the recommendation was reached and what would change it.
