Human-AI Decision Layer
Architecture — Kernel / OS Model
Interface
User-facing
Three calibrated tiers — Expert, Practitioner, Operational — each presenting the same underlying reasoning engine through language and structure appropriate to the user's sophistication level. Same kernel. Different interface.
OS Layer
ProView Framework
Governs how the model's capability is allocated and constrained: truth-seeking stance, epistemic discipline, assumption governance, blast radius classification, decision rights, confidence calibration, anti-sycophancy rules, and audit provenance. The framework makes raw capability purposeful and safe.
Kernel
Foundation Model
Claude Sonnet — raw language capability, pattern recognition, reasoning. Undifferentiated without governance. Prone to sycophancy, silent degradation, and confirmation bias when used without an operating layer.
Four Non-Negotiable Rules — Apply at Every Tier
RULE 01
Truth-Seeking Stance
The model operates as a decision-support tool, never a confirmation tool. No flattery. No mirroring conclusions. Uncertainty stated plainly and confidence reduced accordingly.
RULE 02
Assumption Discipline
All material assumptions flagged explicitly with correction hooks. Outputs clearly distinguish verified facts, estimates, and unknowns. Default to Unknown rather than guessing.
RULE 03
Blast Radius Classification
Every decision classified by reversibility and organizational impact before analysis begins. High blast radius decisions require stronger evidence, staged rollout, and explicit stop conditions.
RULE 04
Decision Rights
Decision owner, accountable owner, consulted stakeholders, and operational burden holders identified at the outset. Misalignment risk flagged when burden falls on teams outside the decision loop.
Interface Tiers — Same Kernel, Calibrated Output
Tier 1
Expert
Senior analysts, CISO, security architects
  • Full ProView structure
  • Calibrated confidence with reasoning
  • Epistemic vocabulary
  • Deep Dive tier available
  • Base rate anchoring explicit
  • Full audit provenance
Tier 2
Practitioner
Mid-level analysts, team leads
  • Simplified decision checklist
  • Plain-language confidence
  • Key assumptions surfaced as questions
  • Standard depth default
  • Blast radius in plain terms
  • Next actions prioritized
Tier 3
Operational
Junior staff, recurring decision types
  • Structured templates only
  • Framework invisible to user
  • Binary or bounded outputs
  • Escalation triggers built in
  • Mini depth only
  • No epistemic vocabulary
Illustrative Output — Vendor Risk Decision Across Tiers
Decision prompt: "Should we extend our EDR vendor contract for 3 years given the current threat landscape?"
Tier 1 — Expert output
"Recommendation: Pilot extension only (12 months). Confidence: 62% — not higher because threat landscape assumptions are 6 months stale and two credible alternatives have emerged. Key assumption: current vendor's AI detection roadmap delivers Q3. Disconfirmer: if CrowdStrike closes the pricing gap by >20%, reassess immediately. Blast radius: high / low reversibility after 18 months."
Tier 2 — Practitioner output
"Leaning toward a shorter extension — here's why: we're assuming their roadmap delivers on time, and that's not confirmed yet. Before committing to 3 years, worth checking if two newer vendors are worth a closer look. Biggest risk is locking in before the market settles. Suggest 12-month bridge with a formal review at 9 months."
Tier 3 — Operational output
"This decision needs senior review before proceeding. Contracts over 2 years with blast radius above threshold require CISO sign-off. Recommended action: escalate to team lead with current vendor performance scorecard attached. Do not renew without completed vendor comparison checklist."
Decision Provenance
High blast radius outputs include a lightweight audit record: depth tier selected and rationale, material assumptions flagged, disconfirmers identified, and decision rights assigned. Designed for post-incident review and board-level accountability without adding friction to routine decisions.
Governance Tags — Every Output
DEPTH: STANDARD BLAST: HIGH REV: LOW CONFIDENCE: 62% OWNER: ASSUMED SEARCH: USED

Provenance tags travel with the output. Any stakeholder can reconstruct how the recommendation was reached and what would change it.
